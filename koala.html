KOALA

Koalas are adorable marsupials native to Australia, known for their distinctive appearance and tree-dwelling habits.

Koalas (Phascolarctos cinereus) are iconic marsupials native to Australia, known for their adorable appearance and unique lifestyle. They are often referred to as "koala bears," although they are not bears at all but rather belong to the marsupial family.

Koalas have several distinctive features, the most notable of which is their large, round face and fluffy ears. They have a stocky body covered in thick, soft fur that helps to insulate them from the cold. Koalas are well adapted to their arboreal lifestyle, with strong limbs and sharp claws for climbing trees. They primarily feed on eucalyptus leaves, which are toxic to most animals, but koalas have a specialized digestive system that allows them to detoxify and extract nutrients from these leaves.

Koalas are herbivorous and can be found in different habitats including savannahs, forests, deserts and marshes. They prefer to stay near water.
