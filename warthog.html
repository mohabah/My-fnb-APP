warthog

Warthogs are wild pigs found in Africa, known for their distinctive appearance and behavior. They belong to the family Suidae and are scientifically classified as Phacochoerus africanus. Warthogs are typically found in savannahs, grasslands, and woodlands, where they can easily access food and water.

Warthogs have several distinctive features, the most notable of which are their large heads, elongated bodies, and tusks. They have a sparse covering of coarse hair and prominent facial warts, which are more pronounced in males. Warthogs are well adapted to their environment, with strong legs for running and digging.
